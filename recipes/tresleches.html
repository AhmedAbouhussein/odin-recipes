Mexican Tres Leches

Mexican tres leches cake in a mexican restaurant. Source: NightCafe

Mexican tres leches cake is a beloved dessert that is known for its rich and indulgent flavors. This classic Mexican dessert consists of a light and fluffy sponge cake that is soaked in a mixture of three milks: evaporated milk, condensed milk, and heavy cream. The cake absorbs the milks, resulting in a moist and tender texture that practically melts in your mouth. Tres leches cake is often topped with whipped cream and garnished with fresh fruit or a sprinkle of cinnamon, adding a touch of sweetness and visual appeal to the dessert. The combination of the moist cake, creamy milk mixture, and delightful toppings make Mexican tres leches cake a true delight for the taste buds.

One of the reasons why Mexican tres leches cake is so popular is its unique and irresistible taste. The combination of the three milks creates a luscious and creamy flavor that is both decadent and comforting. The moistness of the cake, brought about by the milk soaking, adds a luxurious texture that enhances the overall experience. The contrast between the lightness of the cake and the richness of the milks creates a perfect balance that keeps you coming back for more. Whether enjoyed on its own or as part of a festive celebration, Mexican tres leches cake is a culinary masterpiece that delights dessert enthusiasts around the world.

Ingredients

Cake:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon sal
  • 5 large eggs, separated
  • 1 cup granulated sugar, divided
  • 1/3 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Milk mixture:

  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream

Topping:

  • 2 cups whipped cream
  • Cinnamon powder for dusting (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a mixing bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the egg yolks with 3/4 cup of the granulated sugar until pale and creamy.
  4. Add the milk and vanilla extract to the egg yolk mixture and mix well.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the egg yolk mixture and stir until just combined.
  6. In another bowl, beat the egg whites until soft peaks form. Gradually add the remaining 1/4 cup of granulated sugar and continue beating until stiff peaks form.
  7. Gently fold the beaten egg whites into the batter until no streaks remain.
  8. Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and spread it evenly.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  10. While the cake is baking, prepare the milk mixture. In a bowl, whisk together the sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, and heavy cream until well combined.
  11. Once the cake is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.
  12. Using a fork or skewer, poke holes all over the surface of the cake.
  13. Slowly pour the milk mixture over the cake, allowing it to soak in.
  14. Cover the cake and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld and the cake to absorb the milks.
  15. Before serving, spread the whipped cream evenly over the top of the cake.
  16. Optionally, dust the cake with cinnamon powder for added flavor and decoration.
  17. Slice and serve chilled. Enjoy the deliciousness of Mexican tres leches cake!

Note: The cake tastes even better when it's had some time to sit and absorb the milks, so preparing it a day in advance is recommended.
